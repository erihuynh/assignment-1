understanding me

my favorite part of being alive is my room.

i can be anything else during the day, painting on masks that i can hardly even recognize to please people.

yet, between the four walls right beside the staircase– i am me.

i am me in the sense that i am comfortable purely existing.

my existence feels small in the presence of others.

it’s the size of a pea when i’m with my parents.

it’s the size of an orange when i’m with my peers.

it’s the size of the ocean when i’m in my room.

i am me in the sense that it is a reflection of my mind.

on days where i struggle to get up in the morning,

my floor is littered with randomly scattered papers and books.

on brighter days, my drawers are neatly organized and my duvet is nicely tucked into my bed.

i am me in the sense that i am free.

my accelerating heart rate suddenly slows and i am transported to another dimension.

a pressure is lifted and i am weightless.

i don’t know who i am.

i feel lost in crowds of people, my hands clam up, and i feel weak.

i am vulnerable.

i am weak.

when my feet reach the fluffy rug of my room, i know who i am.

i am a girl trying her best.

i am a girl trying to navigate life.

i am a girl who loves her room–

because the feeling of being understood transcends everything.
